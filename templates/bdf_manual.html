📘 BDCOM Devs Forum Manual

This manual helps you understand how to use and contribute to the BDCOM Devs Forum effectively.

📝 Creating a Post

  • Click the “Create” button (right corner of the Navigation bar).
  • Fill in the Title, Type (Query or Patch), Category, and the Content.
  • You can add reference of another post by selecting a Reference ID if needed.
  • Click “Submit” to publish.

🧩 What is Query & Patch?

  • Query is something that you have faced problematic or challenging. You may need help for this. Mainly it means - you are asking for help.
  • On the other hand Patch is a fancy name for Quick Fix or Solution. The solution can either be:
    • In response to a specific Query (i.e., a previously raised problem or issue), or
    • A standalone tip, trick, workaround, or best practice that can help others avoid or resolve similar issues in the future.

📂 Post Categories

When creating a post on BDF, you must select a category. Categories help organize content and make it easier for others to filter and find relevant discussions.

  • VxWorks - For anything related to VxWorks: development, bugs, features, or usage.
  • Linux - For posts about Linux-based Development, tools, kernel issues, scripting, or troubleshooting.
  • Technical - For technical topics that don’t fall under a specific OS. This includes networking, protocols, code snippets, tools, debugging tips, etc.
  • Non Technical - For general or casual discussions that are not directly technical. Use this for community news, announcements, or casual sharing.

Tips:

  • ✅ Choose the most relevant category — it helps others find it faster.
  • 🔄 Admins may re-categorize posts if needed.
  • 🔍 Filtering by category is available on the homepage for focused browsing.

💬 Giving Feedback

  • Open a post and scroll to the Feedback section.
  • Write your feedback and click “Give Feedback”.
  • If it’s your feedback, you can edit or delete it.

🌟 Endorsing Posts

  • Click the “🌟 Endorse” button under any post to show your support or agreement.
  • Each user can endorse a post only once. Clicking the button again will remove your endorsement.
  • In simple term - it's the LIKE button.

📌 Add Reference to Post

  • You can optionally link your post to a previously posted Query or Patch by selecting it from the 🔗 Reference dropdown.
  • This helps others understand the context or origin of your post, especially when submitting a follow-up fix or related discussion.
  • Referencing posts promotes better traceability and keeps related topics connected.
  • You can see whether a post has a reference attached by checking the post detail page—this information is displayed below the post content by Addressing to [post_title]

🔒 User IP Registration

To maintain a personalized and accountable experience in BDCOM Devs Forum, every user must register their IP address along with a preferred username.

📌 Why Register?

  • Associates your posts and comments with a recognizable name.
  • Enables the forum to pre-fill your identity when creating or editing posts.
  • Prevents impersonation by binding usernames to specific IPs within the office network.

🛠️ How It Works

  • When visiting the Create Post page, the system checks your current IP.
  • If your IP is already registered, you'll see a message:
    “Are you John?”
    • Selecting Yes auto-fills your username.
    • Selecting No allows you to input a new name and optionally update the IP registration.
  • If your IP is not registered, you will see a prompt:
    ⚠️ Please register your IP and username. Register here.

🔄 Updating Info

  • If your IP or username changes, visit the IP Management page to update the record.
  • Admins can also delete outdated bindings.
